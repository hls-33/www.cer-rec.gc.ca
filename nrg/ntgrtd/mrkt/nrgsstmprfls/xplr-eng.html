Provincial and Territorial Energy Profiles - Explore

Home Explore Canada YT NT NU BC AB SK MB ON QC NB NL NS PE

These four interactive dashboards explore hydrocarbon production (crude oil and natural gas), electricity generation, energy end-use demand, and greenhouse gas emissions for each province and territory.

The dashboards have a series of filters that allow you to customize region, sector, unit of measure, and the year (if applicable). To remove a filtered view, click on the previously selected element or refresh the web page. If the dashboard uses a map, the map can also be used as a filter, just click on the province or territory. Click on any part of the map outside the country to get back to all of Canada view. Hovering over a data item or the province/territory provides more detail in a tooltip.

These dashboards are updated annually as information becomes available. Please send feedback to energy.markets@cer-rec.gc.ca.

Figure 1: Hydrocarbon Production in Canada

Source and Description:

Source:
Canada’s Energy Future 2018

Description:
This interactive dashboard illustrates natural gas and crude oil production by province or territory in Canada.

In 2017, Alberta produced 10.5 billion cubic feet per day (Bcf/d) of natural gas (67.6% of Canadian total production). British Columbia produced 4.5 Bcf/d (29.0%), Saskatchewan produced 0.4 Bcf/d (2.6%), and Nova Scotia produced 0.1 Bcf/d (0.8%). The remaining jurisdictions (Ontario, New Brunswick, and the Northwest Territories) produced less than 0.1 Bcf/d combined in 2017.

In 2017, Alberta produced 3 538 thousand barrels per day (Mb/d) of crude oil (81.1% of Canadian total production). Saskatchewan produced 485 Mb/d (11.1%), Newfoundland and Labrador produced 221 Mb/d (5.1%), British Columbia produced 75 Mb/d (1.7%) and Manitoba produced 39 Mb/d (0.9%). The remaining jurisdictions (Ontario, Nova Scotia, and the Northwest Territories) produced less than 1 Mb/d combined in 2017.

Figure 2: Electricity Generation in Canada (2017)

Source and Description:

Source:
Canada’s Energy Future 2018

Description:
This interactive dashboard illustrates Canadian electricity generation by jurisdiction and by fuel type in 2017. The data is displayed in gigawatt hours (GW.h).

In 2017, a total of 650 244 GW.h of electricity was generated in Canada. The three largest producers of electricity were Quebec (212 271 GW.h), Ontario (152 011 GW.h), and Alberta (82 384 GW.h).

The largest source of electricity in Canada in 2017 was hydroelectricity (392 665 GW.h), accounting for 60% of Canada’s electricity production. Uranium was the second largest fuel source, accounting for 15% of Canada’s generation (95 389 GW.h). The other fuel sources for electricity generation in Canada were natural gas (57 973 GW.h or 9%), coal and coke (57 631 GW.h or 9%), wind (28 775 GW.h or 4%), biomass (11 777 GW.h or 2%), petroleum (4 000 GW.h or 1%), and solar (2 033 GW.h or <1%).

Figure 3: Canadian End-Use Energy Demand and Energy Intensity Measures (2016)

Source and Description:

Source:
Canada’s Energy Future 2018 and Statistics Canada (Tables 36-10-0222-01 and 17-10-0005-01)

Description:
This interactive dashboard illustrates total end-use energy demand by sector and by jurisdiction in Canada in 2016. The dashboard also displays two energy intensity measures for Canadian provinces: end-use energy demand per capita and end-use energy demand per gross domestic product (GDP).

In 2016, total end-use energy demand in Canada was 11 146 petajoules (PJ). The sector with the largest end-use energy demand was the industrial sector at 5 777 PJ or 51.8% of total end-use demand. The transportation sector was the second largest (2 601 PJ), followed by the residential sector (1 441 PJ) and lastly the commercial sector (1 326 PJ).

The top three largest jurisdictions for energy consumption were Alberta (3 665 PJ), Ontario (3 000 PJ), and Quebec (1 741 PJ).

Alberta is the most energy intensive jurisdiction, consuming 873 gigajoules (GJ) per capita and 12 147 GJ per million $ GDP. The least energy intensive jurisdictions are Nova Scotia (174.0 GJ per capita) and Yukon (2 505 GJ per $ million GDP). The national averages for energy intensity are 307.5 GJ per capita and 5 511 GJ per million $ GDP.

Figure 4: GHG Emissions and Emissions Intensity

Source and Description:

Source:
Environment and Climate Change Canada – National Inventory Report and Statistics Canada (Tables 36-10-0222-01 and 17-10-0005-01)

Description:
This interactive dashboard illustrates greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions by province and by sector. The dashboard also illustrates GHG emissions per capita and per gross domestic product. Data is available for 1990 to 2017.

In 2017, Canada’s total GHG emissions were 715.7 megatonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent (MT of CO2e).

27.2% of Canada’s GHG emissions were from the oil and gas sector. The transportation sector contributed 24.4%, industries and manufacturing contributed 13.2%.
