Pipeline Damage Prevention Regulations – What you need to know

What people living and working near pipelines need to know

Pipeline Damage Prevention Regulations – What you need to know [PDF 408 KB]

The damage prevention regulations help keep people, the environment, and pipelines safe. It is important to know about:

  • The Prescribed Area and the steps to take when working in this area
  • Which activities require the company’s written consent prior to starting the activity

What do I need to do in the planning stages of my activity?

  1. Contact the pipeline company to obtain written consent
  2. Make a locate request to a One-Call Notification Centre (clickbeforeyoudig.com) at least three working days before your activity begins. For vehicle crossings, contact the pipeline company directly
  3. Get written safety practices from the pipeline company that are to be followed during your activities
  4. If you are not doing the work yourself, inform those doing the work on your behalf of their responsibilities and the safety practices that are to be followed

What happens when I make a locate request to a One-Call Notification Centre?

  • 1. The notification centre will:
    • Send a notification to the pipeline company about your activity
  • 2. The pipeline company is required to:
    • Confirm and mark the location of the pipe within 3 business days or a mutually convenient time, provide written safety practices, and explain the meaning of the markings

What if the person who proposed the activity and the pipeline company disagree?

  • Either party can contact the CER for assistance
  • The CER offers an Alternative Dispute Resolution service which is a voluntary and no-cost way for those involved to resolve disputes

Graphic description

Activities that Do Not Require Consent

  • Digging less than 30 cm below the surface (example: planting flowers)
  • Agricultural cultivation less than 45 cm unless notified by the pipeline company

Activities that Require Written Consent

  • Removing earth cover from the Right of Way
  • Ground disturbance 30 cm or more in the Prescribed Area
    (eg. digging holes for fence posts or deck footings)
  • Driving vehicles or mobile equipment on the Right of Way
    (does not include agricultural activities)
  • Agricultural cultivation 45 cm or more
  • Placing a structure on a Right of Way
    (eg. shed, fence, pool, wood pile)

Please note that this information sheet is not intended to replace the Pipeline Damage Prevention Regulations.

To learn more:

Please call 1-800-899-1265 and ask to speak to an Operations Inspector about Damage Prevention, email us at DPInfo@cer-rec.gc.ca, or visit our website and search for Damage Prevention.
